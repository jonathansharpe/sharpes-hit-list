First time ever going to back-to-back games in Seattle, and only the
second time ever (other instance in Houston). 

Mariners extended their streak to 8 with a similarly great pitching
effort but an excellent offensive one, scoring 11 runs on 16 hits.
Cal Raleigh notched his 54th home run, but it was the doubles that did
most of the work, with 7, including 3 from Jorge Polanco who continued
his hot hitting. Most of the damage was done off starter Kyle
Hendricks, who couldn't complete 4 innings and yielded 9 of the 11
runs. 

George Kirby saw Woo's start the previous night and one-upped it,
going 6.1 innings notching 14 strikeouts. See what I mean about the
Angels looking terrible? Yeah Kirby completely dominated them today.
Even Mike Trout (who is admittedly past his prime) struck out 3 times.

While there was little doubt at this point, this was the Mariners 82nd
win of the season, cementing a winning record for the season. Despite
the near-misses in 2021, 2023, and 2024, those were also all winning
seasons, pushing the Mariners winning season streak to 5 in a row,
which was the first time in their history they had managed that. Even
in the 90s when they had their best players in their history, their
down years resulted in finishing below .500. Fortunately, this season
would not result in a losing effort, or even a near miss.
