Mariners continued playing steady with a 29-23 record entering this
game. Their opponent, the Nationals had been rebuilding for several
years but had the Mariners number the past few seasons. 

Logan Evans started this game as well, throwing an outstanding 8
innings of 1 run ball. I was really hoping he would finish the game,
as he was only at 88 pitches after that 8th and the Mariners lead 7-1,
but Dan Wilson decided to put in Eduard Bazardo. I was pretty annoyed
because Bazardo had not figured it out yet and did not want to see him
in the game.

With a final score of 9-1, offense also performed well. Julio
Rodriguez started the scoring immediately with a 2 run bomb, with Cal
Raleigh making it back to back with a solo shot. Cal nabbed another
one later in the 5th, one of his 11 multi-homer games in his record
setting season. All around good victory.
