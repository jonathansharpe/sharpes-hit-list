Now well into September, the Mariners appeared poised to make a run.
They were tied with Houston after this game, with whom they'd been
chasing most of the season. The Astros were only getting progressively
more injured as the season went on, giving the Mariners an opportunity
they needed.

This game was in the midst of a 10-game winning streak (#7) and they
won it in typical Mariners fashion, great starting pitching and timely
hitting. 

Bryan Woo had emerged as the Mariners best starter this season, going
6 innings in literally almost every start, and he made it through 6
today only giving up 2 runs, and notching a career high 13 strikeouts.
The Angels have been bad for a while, but were always annoying to face
and the Mariners would often still lose series to them. However, they
looked much worse here and really helped Woo with that strikeout
total.

Mariners offense took a 2-0 lead in the 1st, and after taking it back
in the 4th at 3-2, they never gave it back with a 5-2 final score.
Jorge Polanco was proving to be an excellent add, doubling in 2 to
score those runs in the 1st. He would prove to be incredibly clutch
during September (and beyond).
