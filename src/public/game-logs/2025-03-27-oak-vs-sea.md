Expectations were high going into 2025, but given the failures of 2023
and 2024 it was unclear if the Mariners would be able to break the
trend of missing the postseason by a game or two.

Most of this game was pretty uneventful. The Mariners failed to score
in the first 6 innings, which is always a bad start to the year,
especially for a team known for their slow starts. Fortunately, it was
only a 1-0 game after 6. The Mariners tied it on a sac fly in the 7th,
but they gave the lead right back in the 8th inning with a solo shot.

The Mariners were able to spark a rally in the bottom of the 8th with
a solo homer of their own from Randy Arozarena, and after a walk by
Luke Raley, took the lead for the first time of the night on a two run
home run by Jorge Polanco. He was a pretty late add to the club, and
after a pretty terrible 2024 season in Seattle, it remained to be seen
if he could return to his career norms, or would become another one of
the seemingly endless players who fail to hit in Seattle.

Andres Munoz recorded a 1-2-3 ninth to seal the first win of many for
the 2025 Mariners.
