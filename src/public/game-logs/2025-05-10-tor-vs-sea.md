Mariners had shaken a 3-7 start to end up at 22-15 entering this game
against the Blue Jays. I decided to attend this game on a whim due to
a work schedule change, so first time for me doing that.

With a more or less constant of being down a pitcher in the rotation
for 2025, rookie Logan Evans stepped up, having a very solid season
and keeping the Mariners in every game he pitched, furthering the
trend of the Mariners developing great starting pitchers. Tonight he
went 5 innings allowing 3 runs, not amazing but easily keeping the
Mariners in the game.

Game was tied 3-3 going into the 8th, when Mariners reliever Carlos
Vargas allowed a leadoff walk followed by a parade of singles to give
the Blue Jays a 6-3 lead which would be the final score. Incredibly,
this would not be the final time I saw the Blue Jays play in Seattle
that year.
